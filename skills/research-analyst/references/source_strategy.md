# Source Strategy Guide

Guidance on which source types to prioritize based on the research subject category.

## By Subject Type

### Company Research

| Source Type | Priority | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Company newsroom / blog | 🔴 High | `[company].com/newsroom`, `/blog`, `/press` |
| Business press | 🔴 High | Reuters, Bloomberg, WSJ, Financial Times |
| Tech press (if tech company) | 🔴 High | TechCrunch, The Verge, Ars Technica |
| Industry trade publications | 🟡 Medium | Sector-specific outlets |
| Investor relations / SEC filings | 🟡 Medium | For public companies |
| Social media / executive posts | 🟢 Low | LinkedIn, X — for color, not primary sourcing |
| Analyst reports | 🟢 Low | Gartner, Forrester (often paywalled) |

### Industry / Sector Research

| Source Type | Priority | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Trade publications | 🔴 High | Sector-specific outlets (e.g., Fierce Healthcare, Finextra) |
| Business press | 🔴 High | Reuters, Bloomberg, WSJ |
| Regulatory / government | 🔴 High | SEC, FDA, FCC, EU Commission — depends on sector |
| Industry associations | 🟡 Medium | Trade group announcements, reports |
| Research firms | 🟡 Medium | McKinsey, Deloitte, BCG (public reports) |
| Conference / event coverage | 🟢 Low | Keynote summaries, event recaps |

### Topic / Technology Research

| Source Type | Priority | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| News outlets covering the topic | 🔴 High | Depends on topic — tech press for tech, health press for health |
| Academic / research sources | 🔴 High | arXiv, PubMed, university announcements |
| Company blogs of major players | 🟡 Medium | Companies building or adopting the technology |
| Community & open-source | 🟡 Medium | GitHub, Hacker News, Reddit (for developer topics) |
| Government / policy sources | 🟢 Low | Relevant when regulation is in play |

### Competitor Intelligence

| Source Type | Priority | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Each competitor's newsroom / blog | 🔴 High | Direct announcements |
| Business press | 🔴 High | Reuters, Bloomberg, TechCrunch |
| Review & comparison sites | 🟡 Medium | G2, Capterra, product review outlets |
| Job postings | 🟡 Medium | Signals for expansion, new capabilities |
| Patent filings | 🟢 Low | Signals for future product direction |
| Social media | 🟢 Low | Executive statements, community sentiment |

---

## General Source Quality Hierarchy

When the same story is covered by multiple sources, prefer in this order:

1. **Primary source** — The company/org that made the announcement
2. **Wire services** — Reuters, AP, Bloomberg (factual, fast)
3. **Major business press** — WSJ, FT, NYT Business
4. **Trade / specialist press** — Deep domain expertise
5. **Tech press** — TechCrunch, The Verge, Ars Technica
6. **Aggregators / blogs** — Use only if no better source available

---

## Paywalled Content

When encountering paywalled sources:
- Use available excerpts and note the limitation
- Search for the same story from a non-paywalled source
- Do NOT present paywalled-source summaries as if the full article was read
