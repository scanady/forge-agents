# AI Domain Taxonomy

Categorization framework for AI developments. Use these categories when organizing research findings. Select 4–7 categories that fit the actual research — skip any category with no findings.

---

## Category Definitions

### 🧠 Frontier Models & Research

**What belongs here**: New model releases from major labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta, Mistral, xAI). Benchmark results and comparisons. Architecture innovations (new attention mechanisms, training techniques). Research papers that advance the state of the art. Scaling results. Multimodal capabilities.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Does this change what's possible? (capability frontier)
- Does this change cost/access? (efficiency frontier)
- How does it compare to existing best-in-class?

**Example items**: GPT-5 release, Claude 4 launch, Llama 4 weights, new SOTA on MMLU, novel training approach from a top lab.

---

### 🛠️ Developer Tools & Infrastructure

**What belongs here**: Frameworks for building AI applications (LangChain, LlamaIndex, CrewAI). Inference engines (vLLM, TGI, Ollama). API platforms and SDKs. MCP servers and tool integrations. Vector databases. Prompt engineering tools. Evaluation frameworks. Deployment and serving infrastructure.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Does this make building AI apps easier/faster?
- Does this reduce cost or improve latency?
- Does it unlock new patterns (agents, RAG, fine-tuning)?

**Example items**: LangChain major release, new MCP server for a popular API, vLLM performance breakthrough, new RAG framework launch.

---

### 💻 Open Source & Community

**What belongs here**: New open-source model releases (weights, quantizations). Community-driven projects gaining traction. Forks and alternatives to proprietary tools. Self-hosted solutions. GitHub trending AI repos. Open datasets. Community benchmarks and leaderboards.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Does this close the gap between open and closed models?
- Is this a credible self-hosted alternative to a paid service?
- What's the community adoption signal? (stars, forks, Reddit buzz)

**Example items**: Open-weight model matching GPT-4 on benchmarks, GGUF quantization of a new model, trending open-source agent framework, community-driven evaluation leaderboard.

---

### 💰 Funding & Business Moves

**What belongs here**: Venture capital rounds in AI companies. Acquisitions and mergers. IPO signals. Major partnerships (e.g., enterprise deals, cloud partnerships). Strategic pivots. Hiring sprees or layoffs at AI companies. Revenue milestones.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Does this signal where smart money is going?
- Does it change the competitive landscape?
- What does it imply about the company's trajectory?

**Example items**: $1B funding round for AI startup, major acquisition by BigTech, AI company IPO filing, enterprise partnership announcement.

---

### 📱 Products & Applications

**What belongs here**: Consumer AI products launching or updating (ChatGPT features, Copilot updates, AI-native apps). Enterprise AI products. Industry-specific AI applications (healthcare, legal, finance). AI features added to non-AI products. Viral AI tools.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Does this change how a meaningful user base works?
- Is this a new category or improvement in an existing one?
- What's the adoption signal? (downloads, virality, enterprise deals)

**Example items**: ChatGPT new feature, Cursor/Windsurf update, AI-powered design tool launch, AI feature in a major enterprise product.

---

### ⚖️ Regulation & Policy

**What belongs here**: Government actions on AI (executive orders, legislation, agency guidelines). International regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act enforcement, China AI rules). AI safety frameworks from industry or government bodies. Legal proceedings involving AI (copyright, liability, antitrust). Standards bodies issuing AI guidance.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Who is affected? (companies, developers, users, specific industries)
- What compliance actions are required?
- What is the enforcement timeline?

**Example items**: New EU AI Act provisions taking effect, US executive order on AI, copyright ruling affecting training data, AI safety framework from a major government.

---

### 🔮 Signals & Emerging Trends

**What belongs here**: Themes gaining momentum that don't fit neatly into one category. Early-stage developments that could become major. Community sentiment shifts. Polymarket odds on AI outcomes. Recurring topics across platforms. Things getting attention for the first time.

**Impact dimensions to assess**:
- Is this a leading indicator of a bigger shift?
- What would confirm or invalidate this trend?
- Who should pay attention to this early?

**Example items**: Agent-based workflows becoming mainstream, "post-prompting" paradigm discussions, AI hardware supply chain concerns, sudden community interest in a previously niche topic.

---

## Cross-Cutting Themes

When categorizing, watch for themes that span multiple categories:

| Theme | May Appear In |
|-------|--------------|
| **AI Agents** | Frontier Models (capability), Dev Tools (frameworks), Products (applications), Open Source (community projects) |
| **Multimodal AI** | Frontier Models (new capabilities), Products (consumer apps), Open Source (community models) |
| **AI Safety / Alignment** | Regulation (policy), Frontier Models (research), Signals (community sentiment) |
| **Cost & Efficiency** | Dev Tools (inference optimization), Open Source (quantization), Frontier Models (pricing changes) |
| **Enterprise Adoption** | Products (launches), Funding (deals), Dev Tools (infrastructure) |

When a development spans themes, place it in the most specific category but note the cross-cutting relevance in the impact assessment.
